Background

FanLabel brings you a unique experience of being able to run your own record label, "sign" artists of your choice, discover new music, partake in daily music contests where you can pick hit songs, earn points, and cash them in for merch!

I joined the UX/UI team here at FanLabel during May of 2019 and during my team here I was responsible for asset designs and the UX/UI design of the "Marketplace", "Profile Page", "Level-Up", "Tournament Leader-boards", "Messages", and "Leagues". The concepting and designing of the "Leagues" system was where I spent most of my time with FanLabel.

Goals

Creating a system that allows players to form groups, facilitating cooperation in order to reach a common goal, further increasing user retention via encouraging group activities.

Problem

Competitive games without some form of cooperation often end up being very hollow experiences with poor user retention. Cooperation or teamplay allows similar minded players or even simply just friends to band together under a common goal, a common name, a common banner in order to play for bragging rights, prizes, and more!

The main problem was deciding on a system that wouldn't feel obsolete and would encourage players to join a League, instead of just staying solo player.

Solution

For this project, I validated the need for the system I had in mind and leveraged data from research and user experience with similar applications.
